Simulation Help Documentation

Game of Life

Description: Conway's Game of Life is a cellular automaton where cells evolve based on simple survival rules.

Rules:

  • A living cell with 2 or 3 neighbors survives.
  • A dead cell with exactly 3 neighbors is born.
  • All other cells die or remain dead.

Spreading of Fire

Description: A simulation of forest fire spread based on neighboring trees and wind conditions.

Rules:

  • A tree burns if at least one neighbor is on fire.
  • Empty spaces remain empty.
  • The fire spreads according to wind direction.

Bacteria

Description: A simulation that models competing bacteria colonies.

Rules:

  • Rock crushes Scissors.
  • Scissors cut Paper.
  • Paper wraps Rock.

Percolation

Description:Simulates fluid flow through a porous material.

Rules:

  • If current state of a cell in blocked, nothing can change its state.
  • If the current state of the cell is percolated, nothing can change its state.
  • If the current state of the cell is open and there is at least one neighbor with percolated state, then its state will be changed to percolated

Langton Loop

Description: A cellular automaton that simulates a self-replicating loop structure.

Rules:

  • The loop consists of a sequence of states that allow it to grow and eventually replicate itself.
  • Cells transition between states based on predefined rules that control the loopâ€™s extension and replication.
  • The loop must have a closed shape to continue replication successfully.
  • Once the replication phase completes, new loops can begin their own cycle of growth.

Wa-Tor World

Description: A predator-prey simulation where sharks hunt fish in an ocean ecosystem.

Rules:

  • Fish move randomly to adjacent empty cells and reproduce after a set number of steps.
  • Sharks move towards fish to eat them and gain energy, otherwise, they lose energy over time.
  • Sharks reproduce after accumulating enough energy.
  • Sharks die if their energy reaches zero.

Foraging Ants

Description: A decentralized system where ants collectively find and retrieve food using pheromone trails.

Rules:

  • Ants start from a nest and explore the environment randomly.
  • When an ant finds food, it carries it back to the nest while leaving a pheromone trail.
  • Other ants are more likely to follow stronger pheromone trails, reinforcing paths to food sources.
  • Pheromones evaporate over time, reducing influence if food sources are exhausted.

Schelling Segregation

Description: A model that simulates how small individual preferences can lead to large-scale segregation.

Rules:

  • Each cell represents an agent belonging to a group (e.g., red or blue).
  • An agent will move to a new location if a certain percentage of its neighbors are not the same type.
  • If an agent is satisfied with its surroundings, it remains in place.
  • Over time, this behavior results in clusters of like-minded agents forming, leading to segregation.

Sugar Scape

Description: A multi-agent system that models resource consumption, reproduction, and social behaviors.

Rules:

  • The grid contains patches of sugar, which agents consume for survival.
  • Each agent has a metabolism rate and consumes sugar over time.
  • Agents move toward locations with the highest sugar availability.
  • Sugar regenerates over time, allowing for long-term resource sustainability.
  • Some versions include wealth accumulation, trade, and reproduction among agents.

Rules-Based Game of Life

Description: A cellular automaton where cells evolve based on customized rules beyond the traditional Conway's Game of Life.

Rules:

  • Each cell is either alive or dead.
  • The state of a cell in the next step depends on user-defined rules.
  • Rules may include survival, reproduction, or decay conditions based on neighboring cells.
  • Variations may introduce new states, growth patterns, or environmental influences.
  • Custom rule sets allow for experimentation with different emergent behaviors.
